Here's the part most people never think about: the hard bit isn't *calling* a store, it's getting past the phone tree to an actual person. Call a pharmacy or a big-box store yourself and you'll burn five minutes in a menu before anyone picks up.

We solved that by **mapping the phone systems of the national chains**, so Check AI knows the fastest route to a human before it even dials.

- **Keypad menus** ("press 3 for the store"): we press the right keys the instant we hear the menu, automatically.
- **Voice menus** (the kind that asks you to *say* what you want, like a pharmacy line): we speak the right words to get routed straight through.
- **The toughest trees:** we've learned and saved the exact path to a real person for each chain, and reuse it on every call, so it only gets faster.

The payoff: on a mapped chain a real person is on the line in **seconds, not minutes**, which is the difference between a check that's worth running and one that isn't. It's also why we can promise **no answer = no charge**: we've made reaching a human cheap and reliable enough to eat the cost of the calls that don't.
