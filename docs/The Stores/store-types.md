---
title: "Store types & what they mean for price"
hidden: false
---

Not every store is the same kind of bet. The type tells you whether you're likely to find genuine product at list price, or roll the dice.

- **Retail** (Target, Walmart, CVS, Barnes & Noble), sealed, genuine product at or near list price. The best-value hunt, and the highest-rated.
- **Card shop**, local hobby shops. Real sealed product, but almost always priced above MSRP. Great selection, you pay for it.
- **Thrift** (Goodwill, Savers), a treasure hunt for loose, used cards from collections. Spotty by nature, kept as its own separate experience.
- **Kiosk**, the official Pokémon vending machines. A machine sets the price, so it's always exact MSRP, the best possible target when one's near you.

Under the hood these roll up from a much finer grid, dollar & discount, pharmacy, grocery, warehouse club, electronics, sporting goods, and more, which is how we can point you at the *right* shelf instead of just the nearest one.
