title: Your verdict
---
Every check comes back as a short verdict. The color tells the story before you read a word.

| | Verdict | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 | **In stock** | They've got it. Go grab it before it's gone. |
| 🟠 | **Restock incoming** | Not on the shelf yet, but staff told us when the truck lands. |
| 🔴 | **Not in / Sold out** | Save the trip. Covers "sold out" and "they don't carry it." |
| 🟡 | **Couldn't tell** | The answer wasn't clear. Read the convo and you decide. No charge. |
| ⚪ | **No answer** | Nobody picked up, or we couldn't reach a person. No charge. |

Those five colors cover a longer list under the hood. "Couldn't tell" wraps up things like left on hold, too busy to check, or a language barrier. "No answer" wraps up voicemail, a busy line, a wrong number, or a closed store. The rule never changes: **you only pay on a clear yes or no.**

Under the verdict you get the receipt: the real back and forth between Check AI and the store. You're never taking our word for it. If the store says a shipment landed this morning, you'll see them say it.
