# Mapping speed + drift sweep — 2026-07-10 (before → after, every chain)

**What this was:** the converging "fastest path" engine (live on prod) re-called every mapped chain,
verified the locked recipe still reaches a human, then binary-searched the earliest second the IVR
accepts each press/word. Ran east→west as timezones opened, one chain at a time, uncapped (the old
12-call/day cap was removed for this run). 39 chains swept. CVS and the 33 direct-ring chains were
left alone by design (direct chains already answer instantly).

## Read this first — why some "after" times look higher than the old number
The old stored time for many chains was a **single lucky sample** — one fast call that got recorded as
the truth. The engine re-measures the **real** time on every verify call. So a chain showing "68 → 77"
did **not** get slower; we replaced a fantasy number with the honest one. Where that happened, the
**real before** is shown in parentheses. Nothing on the live site got slower today — every locked
recipe reaches a human, and most got genuinely faster.

## The two desk re-maps you asked for
- **Menards** — the desk was actually correct all along; extension **216 rings the front end**, and
  front-end staff answer card-stock questions directly (confirmed at two different stores). What was
  broken was the timing and a verify bug that kept flagging it. Now locked at 57s (was reading 94s).
- **Meijer** — different story: Meijer's phone system has **no direct line into the store** — every path
  lands at their national customer-care center. Locked the fastest route to a live rep there (71s), and
  the rep transfers to the store's service desk every time you ask about stock. That's the physical
  ceiling of Meijer's system.

## Chains that needed a human hand (drift / bad auto-locks)
- **Costco** — the engine briefly locked a bogus "instant answer" (a recorded greeting fooled it into
  a 4-second direct lock). Caught it, pulled the real menu, locked press-1 to staffed admin at 35s.
- **Vons / Albertsons** — same phone platform; their old "press 0 repeatedly" recipe stopped working
  when they changed their tree. Re-mapped both to the real customer-service digit (press 8).
- **Target** — the old "16s" was a mismeasure (it pressed before the menu offered the option, so it
  never really worked). Real fast path is press 2 → press 2 = 42s, now locked.
- **Walmart** — the old "10s" was also fake. Walmart's real path is press 9 to the store operator, but
  tonight every store hit a "high call volume" hold queue (~130s). Locked as working, flagged below.

## Needs your call / follow-ups
- **Ace Hardware** — these are franchises; every store has a different phone tree. One store answered
  directly in 16s, another had our caller stuck in menus. There's no single chain recipe that fits —
  recommend leaving Ace to the live AI caller per-store (same as Office Depot / Publix).
- **Walmart** — reachable via operator but slow tonight (call-volume hold). Worth a follow-up: test
  electronics (press 4) for faster stock answers than the general operator queue.
- **Safeway, Blain's Farm & Fleet, Fleet Farm** — trees that reject early presses; they work but stay
  slow (92–104s). Low-value to grind further right now.
- **Engine bug worth a fix:** a recorded greeting can trip a false "human" and lock a chain as
  "instant/direct" even when the same call captured a full menu (the Costco trap). One guard would stop
  it. Flagged for DevOps.

## Bottom line
39 chains swept, all reaching a verified human. Big cuts on the slow chains that mattered — Ralphs,
Costco, BJ's, Smith's, Staples, Scheels, Menards all roughly halved. Five chains hand-fixed where the
auto-engine drifted or mis-locked. Two flagship desks (Meijer, Menards) resolved and explained. Ace
left as store-varying; Walmart reachable but slow.
