---
title: "No answer = no charge"
hidden: false
---

This isn't a marketing line, it's how the system is wired. A check only costs you something when the call ends in a **definitive** answer, a clear in-stock, not-in-stock, sold-out, or doesn't-carry.

Every other outcome is free, automatically: nobody answered, the line was busy, they put us on hold too long, they were slammed and couldn't check, a language barrier, a bad number, the store turned out closed, or the two reads disagreed. If we didn't get you a straight answer, you keep your check.

> **You pay for answers, not attempts.**
>
> The charge is decided by the server after the call, on the verdict alone, there's no way for a dead-end call to quietly cost you.
