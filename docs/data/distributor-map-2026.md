# Distributor & Category-Management Map for Trading-Card, Collectible, Blind-Box & Novelty-Toy Aisles at Major U.S. Retail Chains

## TL;DR

- **Trading cards/collectibles at mass, drug, grocery, club, dollar, bookstore, craft and pop-culture chains are dominated by two category-management distributors — Excell Brands (Des Moines, IA) and MJ Holding (Bridgeview, IL)** — with Excell holding the Target relationship and a broad confirmed account list (Target, Walmart, Kroger, Meijer, Fred Meyer, CVS, Walgreens, Five Below, BJ’s, H-E-B, Aldi, Barnes & Noble, BoxLunch, Hot Topic, Hobby Lobby, Claire’s), and MJ Holding running Walmart/Target/Meijer/Walgreens plus the repack/“mystery box” business.
- **The Pokémon Company International (TPCi) is acquiring Excell Brands (definitive agreement announced Feb 19, 2026)** — vertically integrating its “largest distribution partner in the U.S.” — and TPCi separately runs its own grocery vending kiosks (Kroger/Albertsons/Safeway/H-E-B families), a TPCi-DIRECT channel that must be tracked separately from the shelf supply Excell/MJ handle.
- **Plush and novelty/fidget lines bypass card distributors and ship direct from the brand owner:** Schylling (NeeDoh) and Jazwares/Kellytoy (Squishmallows) sell directly to their retail networks; blind-box leader Pop Mart (Labubu) uses a direct model (own stores, Robo Shops, website) with NO public mass-retail distributor, and Sonny Angel (Dreams USA) sells direct plus Urban Outfitters and MINISO.

## Key Findings

1. **Excell Brands is the anchor trading-card category manager.** Its own site lists brand partners Pokémon TCG, Disney Lorcana, Magic: The Gathering, Yu-Gi-Oh!, One Piece, Funko, Topps, Panini, Upper Deck, MLB/NFL/NBA/NHL/MLS, plus The Fairfield Company (repacks), Bulls-i-Toy, CardGuard, Disney, Marvel and Sanrio. Confirmed retail partners on Excell’s own website (retrieved June 2026): Target, Walmart, Fred Meyer, Meijer, CVS, Walgreens, Five Below, Barnes & Noble, Aldi, BJ’s, H-E-B, Kroger, BoxLunch, Claire’s, Hobby Lobby, Hot Topic, plus the .com properties and Amazon. 
1. **TPCi → Excell acquisition (Feb 19, 2026).** Per TPCi’s press release, The Pokémon Company International signed a definitive agreement to acquire Excell Brands, stating: “Excell Brands has become The Pokémon Company International’s largest distribution partner in the U.S.” TPCi President Kenji Okubo: “This reflects our long-term commitment to supporting the Pokémon community and the broader trading card ecosystem.” The deal is pending regulatory approval; both companies mark their 30th anniversaries in 2026; Excell will keep operating independently. It builds on TPCi’s 2022 purchase of printer Millennium Print Group.
1. **MJ Holding is the #2 mass distributor and the repack king.** Founded 1993 (formerly Beckett Associates), 7,000+ retail locations across the U.S. and Canada, headquartered in Bridgeview, IL. Its trading-card distribution business began at Meijer and Walmart (Dec 2000). It serves Walmart (U.S. & Canada), Target, Meijer and Walgreens. It runs the repack/“mystery box” business (Mystery Power Box, Power Cube, Mega Mystery Box) and in May 2025 launched CGC-graded “Mystery Gems” (Pokémon) and “Gems of the Game” (sports) blind boxes at Walmart.  It also distributes newer items like Riftbound blisters and PIGGY minifigure mystery packs at Walmart.
1. **Repacks come from two sources, both tied to the big distributors.** Repacked “value packs”/“mystery boxes” (older, marked-up product) at Walmart/Target/Meijer are distributed by MJ Holding and, since 2024, by The Fairfield Company  — and Fairfield is a division of Excell Brands. These are NOT standard MSRP product and warrant their own SKU class.
1. **Plush/fidget/novelty lines ship direct from brand owners, not card distributors.** Schylling (NeeDoh) and Jazwares/Kellytoy (Squishmallows) are vertically integrated manufacturers who sell directly into their own retail networks.
1. **Pokémon grocery kiosks are TPCi-direct, separate from shelf supply.** TPCi owns/operates over 1,400 Automated Retail Vending Machines across more than 25 U.S. states (early 2026; densest in Texas 40+, California 22+, Washington 15+), placed in Kroger-family, Albertsons-family and H-E-B grocery stores and stocked directly by TPCi at MSRP — distinct from the shelf cards distributed by Excell/MJ.
1. **Specialty hobby shops (and independent vending operators) source through GTS Distribution and Southern Hobby Supply**, not the mass category managers — relevant for local-game-store and GameStop-adjacent channels.

## Details

### 1) DISTRIBUTOR TABLE

**EXCELL BRANDS** (Des Moines, IA; founded 1996)

- *Product lines:* Pokémon TCG, Disney Lorcana, Magic: The Gathering, Yu-Gi-Oh!, One Piece Card Game; Topps, Panini, Upper Deck sports cards; MLB/NFL/NBA/NHL/MLS licensed product; Funko; Sanrio, Disney, Marvel collectibles; The Fairfield Company repacks; Bulls-i-Toy; CardGuard accessories.
- *Retailer network (per Excell’s own site):* Target (described in acquisition coverage as an “exclusive distribution relationship”), Walmart, Kroger, Fred Meyer, Meijer, CVS, Walgreens, Five Below, Barnes & Noble, Aldi, BJ’s Wholesale, H-E-B, BoxLunch, Claire’s, Hobby Lobby, Hot Topic; plus Target.com, Walmart.com, BarnesandNoble.com and Amazon.
- *Channel type:* mass, drugstore, grocery, dollar/value, warehouse club, bookstore, craft/hobby, specialty/pop-culture, e-commerce.
- *Recent shifts (2024–2026):* **Acquired by The Pokémon Company International (definitive agreement announced Feb 19, 2026);** added Upper Deck, Funko, MLS, NHL and Five Below/BJ’s/Hobby Lobby/Barnes & Noble logos to its public roster across 2025.

**MJ HOLDING (MJ Holding Company, LLC)** (Bridgeview, IL; trading-card business since 1993; formerly Beckett Associates)

- *Product lines:* gaming TCGs, sports and entertainment cards, trading-card supplies, collectibles, toys and “hot trend items”; repack/mystery products (Mystery Power Box, Power Cube, Mega Mystery Box); CGC-graded “Mystery Gems”/“Gems of the Game”; Riftbound blisters; PIGGY minifigure packs; toy arm Grin Studios.
- *Retailer network:* Walmart (U.S. & Canada), Target, Meijer, Walgreens; 7,000+ stores total.
- *Channel type:* mass, drugstore, grocery (selective).
- *Recent shifts:* CGC blind-box partnership launched at Walmart (May 2025); competes with Fairfield on repacks; distributes new TCGs (Riftbound) at mass.

**THE FAIRFIELD COMPANY** (a division of Excell Brands)

- *Product lines:* sports-card and TCG repacks/mystery boxes (e.g., Magic value packs, “Hot Corner Box,” “Collector’s Edge Box”).
- *Retailer network:* Walmart, Target, Meijer (mass channel).
- *Channel type:* mass.
- *Recent shifts:* Took over distribution of several Magic resale/mystery products in 2024 (previously MJ Holding). 

**SCHYLLING** (North Andover, MA; founded 1975)

- *Product lines:* NeeDoh (Groovy Glob, Nice Cube, Cool Cats, Dream Drop, etc.), plus classic/nostalgic toys (Lava Lamp, Sea-Monkeys, Big Wheel).
- *Retailer network:* Target, Walmart, CVS, Walgreens, Five Below, Scheels, Cracker Barrel, Barnes & Noble, Hallmark,  Staples, Ace Hardware, plus thousands of independent toy stores; Amazon and Schylling.com online.
- *Channel type:* mass, drugstore, dollar/value, sporting goods, gift, bookstore, hardware, specialty toy.
- *Recent shifts:* NeeDoh went viral late 2025–early 2026. Schylling CEO Paul Weingard told Business Insider (2026): “Literally, within the first nine weeks of the year, we’d sold through the whole year’s inventory. No company can plan for that. It’s been fantastic, overwhelming demand that just well outstrips our ability to replenish.” This caused 8–10-week shipment backlogs, widespread sell-outs and counterfeit warnings.

**JAZWARES / KELLYTOY** (Sunrise, FL; a Berkshire Hathaway company; Kellytoy acquired 2020)

- *Product lines:* Squishmallows (plush), plus Pokémon plush, Star Wars, CoComelon, Fortnite, AEW, Hello Kitty, Adopt Me!; Squishmallows trading cards via licensee Parkside.
- *Retailer network:* Walmart, Target, Costco, Walgreens, CVS, Kroger, Five Below, Hot Topic, GameStop, Books-A-Million, Aldi, Claire’s, Party City, Cracker Barrel, Hallmark, Learning Express, plus shop.jazwares.com and Amazon.
- *Channel type:* mass, club, drugstore, grocery, dollar/value, pop-culture, bookstore, gift, specialty.
- *Recent shifts:* Retailer-exclusive “Squads” at Target, Walgreens, Costco and Great Wolf Lodge; continued lifestyle-brand licensing expansion.

**THE POKÉMON COMPANY INTERNATIONAL (TPCi) — DIRECT** (vending channel)

- *Product lines:* sealed Pokémon TCG (booster packs, ETBs, tins, collection boxes) at MSRP.
- *Retailer network (kiosks):* Kroger family (Kroger, King Soopers, Fred Meyer, Fry’s, Smith’s, Pick ‘n Save, QFC), Albertsons family (Albertsons, Safeway, Vons, Acme, Shaw’s, Star Market, Tom Thumb, Randalls, Jewel-Osco), H-E-B; also limited-time merch deals with Target and GameStop.
- *Channel type:* grocery (kiosk), direct.
- *Recent shifts:* Per OriginalPricing (2026): “In June 2023, there were just 45 machines nationwide. By October 2023, that number hit 120. By early 2024, The Pokémon Company had nearly doubled the count to 200 machines and expanded into California for the first time.” Now over 1,400 machines across 25+ states (early 2026). TPCi is also acquiring Excell (shelf distribution) in 2026.

**GTS DISTRIBUTION** (Everett, WA; merged 2005 from Talkin’ Sports + Gamus)

- *Product lines:* Pokémon and other TCGs, sports/entertainment cards, games, toys, hobby supplies (Ultra Pro, Ultimate Guard, Gamegenic). 
- *Retailer network:* wholesale to independent hobby/game/card shops and to independent vending operators; not a mass-retail category manager.
- *Channel type:* specialty/hobby wholesale.

**SOUTHERN HOBBY SUPPLY** (Nashville, TN + St. Louis, Chicago, Long Island, Reno)

- *Product lines:* Topps, Panini, Upper Deck sports cards; non-sport and gaming cards; supplies. One of only three authorized Upper Deck U.S. distributors. 
- *Retailer network:* wholesale to hobby shops/local card stores nationwide.
- *Channel type:* specialty/hobby wholesale.

**POP MART (Pop Mart North America) — DIRECT**

- *Product lines:* blind-box designer toys — The Monsters/Labubu, Molly, Skullpanda, Dimoo, Crybaby, Hirono; licensed collabs (Sanrio, Disney).
- *Retailer network:* Pop Mart’s own U.S. stores, Robo Shops (vending), website, app and TikTok Shop. Target.com carries official first-party Pop Mart listings; Hot Topic sells Pop Mart per its own catalog; Walmart offers Labubu only via the StockX third-party marketplace  (resale, not shelf). No publicly named mass-retail master distributor.
- *Channel type:* direct retail + selective first-party retail accounts.
- *Recent shifts:* Per Pop Mart FY2025 results (reported via Guangming Online, Mar 27, 2026), year-end 2025 global totals were 630 retail stores and 2,637 vending machines, with North America at “72 stores currently in operation and more than 100 expected by the end of 2026” (Times Square and Fifth Avenue flagships opening Q4 2025). Per CNBC (Oct 23, 2025), Q3 2025 revenue “more than tripled year over year as sales in the U.S. swelled between 1,265% and 1,270%.”

**DREAMS USA (Sonny Angel) — DIRECT + selective**

- *Product lines:* Sonny Angel blind-box mini-figures, Smiski.
- *Retailer network:* SonnyAngelUSA.com direct; Urban Outfitters (primary U.S. stockist);  MINISO stores.
- *Channel type:* direct + specialty/fashion + variety retail.

**MINISO USA — own-brand + Sonny Angel reseller**

- *Product lines:* own-brand blind boxes (Sanrio, Disney, Pixar, Care Bears collabs), plush, Sonny Angel, Smiski. Does NOT sell Pop Mart/Labubu (direct competitor).
- *Retailer network:* 270+ MINISO U.S. stores (end 2025); first U.S. store opened 2017 near Los Angeles.
- *Channel type:* own-store specialty.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Auto-apply only HIGH-confidence links now.** Stamp the chains where a distributor link is documented by the distributor’s own site or a primary/trade source: all Excell accounts on Excell’s logo wall (Target, Walmart, Kroger, Fred Meyer, Meijer, CVS, Five Below, B&N, Aldi, BJ’s, H-E-B, BoxLunch, Claire’s, Hobby Lobby, Hot Topic); MJ Holding at Walmart/Target/Meijer/Walgreens; Schylling and Jazwares across their named networks; TPCi kiosks at grocery. Apply Pokémon-vending as a SEPARATE flag from shelf cards.

**Stage 2 — Hold MED/LOW links for manual review.** Do NOT auto-stamp: Albertsons/Safeway shelf cards, Publix, Hy-Vee, Wegmans, Giant Eagle, Dollar Tree, Family Dollar, Books-A-Million cards, Spencer’s, Michaels, Macy’s, H Mart, 99 Ranch, Tractor Supply/Menards. Mark Rite Aid and JOANN as inactive/closing.

**Stage 3 — Treat repacks and kiosks as distinct SKU classes.** Tag MJ Holding/Fairfield repack/“mystery box” product separately from MSRP sealed product (different pricing logic). Tag TPCi vending product separately from shelf product (MSRP, TPCi-direct, grocery-only).

**Thresholds that change the mapping:** When the TPCi–Excell acquisition closes (regulatory approval pending as of Feb 2026), re-verify whether Excell’s non-Pokémon TCG lines (Lorcana, Magic, Yu-Gi-Oh!, One Piece) or any retail accounts shift. If Pop Mart announces a U.S. mass-retail distributor or a chain announces an official Pop Mart program (beyond Target.com/Hot Topic), upgrade those links. If a chain’s planogram supplier changes (e.g., Walmart consolidating to one card category manager), re-stamp.

## Caveats

- **Excell’s retail “logo wall” is marketing, not a contract list** — it confirms a relationship exists but not which categories or how exclusive. The Target “exclusive distribution relationship” is reported by a fan-news outlet (Bulbagarden) citing the acquisition; treat the exclusivity claim as MED-HIGH, not absolute.
- **Many chain↔distributor links could not be sourced** (Publix, Hy-Vee, Wegmans, Giant Eagle, Dollar Tree, Family Dollar, Spencer’s, Michaels, H Mart, 99 Ranch, Macy’s, most farm/ranch and sporting-goods chains). These are flagged LOW and must NOT be auto-applied.
- **Pop Mart distribution into mass retail is widely misreported by SEO content farms** (alibaba.com “smartbuy” pages, etc.) that fabricate executive quotes and “partnerships” (e.g., a claimed MINISO×Pop Mart deal, a Kroger Pop Mart program). These are false; rely only on Modern Retail/Bloomberg/CNBC/retailer catalogs.
- **The repack distributor split (MJ Holding vs. Fairfield) is evolving** — sourcing shows Fairfield took over some Magic resale products in 2024 while MJ continues its own mystery-box lines; both appear at the same chains.
- **Costco/Sam’s Club card sourcing is inferred** (club-exclusive bundles, direct buys) rather than documented through a named category manager; flagged MED.
- **Pop Mart store-count figures vary by source and date:** a 2025 Bloomberg interview cited 21 U.S. stores mid-2025 targeting 40+ by end-2025, while Pop Mart’s FY2025 results (Guangming Online, Mar 27, 2026) report 72 North America stores at year-end 2025 with 100+ expected by end-2026. The FY2025 figure is the more authoritative.
- Facts are dated where known; the TPCi–Excell deal (Feb 19, 2026) had not closed as of this report (June 25, 2026).
