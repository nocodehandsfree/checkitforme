What a "check" is

A check is one call to one store about one thing. It's the unit you spend. "Free check," "1 check," "checks left", that's the currency.

Your first check is free, no card needed. After that you either buy checks as you go or get a monthly bundle on a plan. A check only gets spent when we come back with a real yes or no. Everything else is on the house (see No answer = no charge).

One check. One store. One straight answer.The word "call" is the literal phone call we place. The word "check" is what you spend. When in doubt, you think in checks.

The stores

The store universe

Behind every check is a national map of over 100,000 real store locations, grouped under roughly 80 core retail brands, plus card shops, thrift stores, and the Pokémon vending machines. When you open the app, that's the haystack we search for the needle near you.

It's built on one rule that keeps everything honest: there's a single source of truth. Every store you see is read live from one database, no screen keeps its own private list, no hardcoded names, no stale spreadsheet. When a store gets fixed, it's fixed everywhere at once.

Stores vs. brands

A brand (a "chain") is Target, or CVS, or Barnes & Noble, it carries the logo, the reliability rating, and how its phone system works. A store is one physical Target on one street, its address, its hours, its own confirmed restock day. Anything true for the whole brand is inherited; anything local lives on the store.

Store types & what they mean for price

Not every store is the same kind of bet. The type tells you whether you're likely to find genuine product at list price, or roll the dice.

Retail MSRP

Target, Walmart, CVS, Barnes & Noble. Sealed, genuine product at or near list price. The best-value hunt, and the highest-rated.

Card shop markup

Local hobby shops. Real sealed product, but almost always priced above MSRP. Great selection, you pay for it.

Thrift used

Goodwill, Savers, and the like. A treasure hunt for loose, used cards from collections. Spotty by nature, kept as its own separate experience.

Kiosk exact MSRP

The official Pokémon vending machines. A machine sets the price, so it's always exact MSRP, the best possible target when one's near you.

How we rank stores

The rating answers one question: how reliably is it worth checking this store for genuine product at MSRP right now? It's a best chance, never a promise. A small, predictable restocker beats a big random one.

There's a separate thing we track that never shows up as a star rating: reach rate, whether a human actually picks up the phone. A Barnes & Noble answers direct; a pharmacy hides behind a phone bot. That's about how hard the store is to reach, not how good the shelf is, and it's what we quietly work to get faster.

Kiosks, the Pokémon vending machines

These are the official TPCi vending machines, usually tucked inside a supermarket. They dispense sealed Pokémon product at exact MSRP, with no staff and no markup, which is why a kiosk store is always rated Best Chance.

There's one hard rule: a store only counts as a kiosk if it's on the official machine list. If it's not on that list, it's not a kiosk, full stop. That keeps the map from filling up with wishful thinking.

A check on a kiosk asks a different question than a shelf check. There's nobody to ask about a shipment, so the call just confirms the machine is powered on and stocked, because they go down, get switched off, and run dry on their own cycles. Shoppers help keep them honest too: forward a machine's email receipt and it's hard proof it was working at that moment (and it earns you credit).

Under the hood

The life of a check

Here's what actually happens between your tap and your verdict. It's more machinery than it looks.

1

You start it

You sign in with your phone number (a texted code, no passwords ever). You pick a store and a product, and that kicks off the check.

2

We dial

The call goes out over the real phone network, and it can even show the store your own verified number, so it's a normal local call, not a robocall.

3

We get to a person

Most stores answer with a menu or a phone tree. Check AI knows the fastest route through each one and works the menu to a human, without burning money doing it (see below).

4

We ask

Once a person is on the line, Check AI asks the one stock question and listens. The whole exchange streams to your phone live.

5

We read the answer twice

Two independent reads decide the verdict. If they disagree, we don't guess, you get an honest "couldn't tell," and it's free.

Meet Check AI

Check AI is the voice that makes the call. It sounds like a friendly person calling around, because that's the job, a store associate is far more helpful to a real "hey, quick question" than to a robot.

The clever part is how it gets to that associate. Every store's phone system is one of three kinds, and Check AI handles each differently:

  • Direct, a person just picks up. Nothing to navigate.
  • Keypad menu, "press 3 for the store." We press the right keys automatically, for free.
  • Voice menu, the kind that wants you to say "front desk." We speak the right words to get through, cheaply.

It learns each store's fastest path once and reuses it, so the calls get quicker and cheaper over time.

Why reaching a person is the real cost

A phone call is cheap. A phone call with a smart AI listening and talking the whole time is not, and while you're just sitting in a phone menu, that smart part is doing nothing useful but still running the meter.

So we keep it switched off until it's needed. Check AI navigates the menu with free keypad presses or cheap prerecorded speech, and the expensive, conversational part only wakes up the moment a human actually answers. The deeper the phone tree, the more we save, because those are exactly the minutes you'd otherwise be paying full price for.

Why it matters to you

This is what makes "no answer = no charge" affordable. We spend almost nothing getting to the person, so we can eat the cost of every call that doesn't reach one.

Proof, not guesses

Stock-checker websites lie constantly, they say "in stock" when the shelf is bare, because their inventory feed is hours behind reality. A phone call to a person standing near the shelf isn't.

That's why every check comes with the receipt: the live transcript of the actual conversation. You see the question, you see the answer, you see the store say a shipment landed this morning. If we tell you it's in stock, you can read exactly why we think so.

Plans & trust

Checks & billing

Your first check is free. After that, you choose how you spend:

  • Pay as you go, buy a pack of checks, use them whenever. They don't expire.
  • A monthly plan, a bundle of checks each month, plus extras like restock alerts, auto-checks on shipment days, and zone sweeps that check all your stores in one tap.
  • Check+, the membership, for the people who hunt seriously.

Billing is handled entirely on our side and only ever fires on a real, answered verdict. You never get charged mid-call or on a maybe.

No answer = no charge

This isn't a marketing line, it's how the system is wired. A check only costs you something when the call ends in a definitive answer, a clear in-stock, not-in-stock, sold-out, or doesn't-carry.

Every other outcome is free, automatically: nobody answered, the line was busy, they put us on hold too long, they were slammed and couldn't check, a language barrier, a bad number, the store turned out closed, or the two reads disagreed. If we didn't get you a straight answer, you keep your check.

You pay for answers, not attempts.The charge is decided by the server after the call, on the verdict alone, there's no way for a dead-end call to quietly cost you.

Privacy & terminology

We keep it lean. Sign-in is your phone number and a texted code, no passwords to leak. We store what's needed to place your checks and show your history, and nothing we don't use.

A couple of words we use carefully, so the app always reads the same way:

  • Check, what you spend, and one call about one product.
  • Call, the literal phone call we place to the store.
  • Check AI, the voice that makes the call.
  • Staff, the person who answers at the store.
